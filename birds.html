Macaw

Life Expectancy: 30-50 years

Loyalty: Very High

Temperament: Intelligent, Playful

Size: Large

▼

The Macaw and called a parrot, is a smart and colorful bird. Also known for their ability to mimic sounds. tropical North and South America are home for these beautiful creatures. Their average lifespan is between 30 to 50 years. They eat fresh fruits and vegetables, nuts. Spending a lot of time with macaws can help you to build a strong bond with them. Macaws can bring happiness and companionship for many years if properly cared for and loved.

Sulphur-crested Cockatoos

Life Expectancy: 40-70 years

Loyalty: High

Temperament: Intelligent, loving

Size: Medium to large

▼

The Sulphur-crested Cockatoo is a beautiful white bird with a unique yellow crest that distinguishes them. The average lifespan for these Cockatoo is around 40 to 70 years. Which makes them perfect for long term companion. They eat fresh fruits, vegetables and the occasional nut. With proper care with love, and attention, Sulphur-crested Cockatoos can become beloved pets for a lifetime.

Canary

Life Expectancy: 8-15 years

Loyalty: Moderate

Temperament: Calm, Singers

Size: Small

▼

Canaries which are native to the Canary Islands, are small songbirds with bright colors. Their average lifespan is between 8 to 15 years. Canaries are granivorous, they eat mostly seeds, but they also eat fresh fruits and vegetables. These lovely birds bring joy with their beautiful songs and charming personalities when given a clean cage and have been given a really good care of them.

Budgerigar

Life Expectancy: 5-8 years

Loyalty: Moderate

Temperament: Social, Lively Active

Size: small

▼

Budgerigars, also known as Budgies or Parakeets, are small and colorful parrots native to Australia. Their average lifespan of 5 to 8 years, though some can live longer. Budgerigars eat seeds, but they also enjoy fresh fruits and vegetables and treats on occasion. With their playful nature and ability to mimic sounds and words when they are comfortable and safe, these social birds make wonderful companions.
